        <h2 class="section-title">PhD Thesis</h2>
        
        <div class="publications-container" style="max-width: 800px; margin: 0 auto;">
            <div class="pub-item">
                <h3 class="pub-title">Modeling Fluid Intelligence via Real-Time Adaptation</h3>
                <p class="pub-meta">Ph.D. Thesis, ELSC, The Hebrew University of Jerusalem, 2025</p>
                <p>This thesis explores real-time adaptation as a computational framework for understanding how minds solve genuinely novel problems. I demonstrate that neural networks can perform abstract reasoning through test-time parameter adaptation—without extensive pre-training—and provide a mechanistic account of paradoxical findings in human belief updating. The work suggests that fluid intelligence emerges from processes where inference and learning occur simultaneously.</p>

                <p class="pub-meta">T. Barak, Y. Loewenstein. Scientific Reports, 2024.</p>
                <p>We tested an ANN's ability to extract a predictably changing feature from image sequences. The ANN performed well on intelligence tests requiring this ability, validating its use in modeling the extraction of abstract theories.</p>
